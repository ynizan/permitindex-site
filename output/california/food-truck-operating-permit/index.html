Food Truck Operating Permit

Annual permit required for mobile food facilities operating in California. Any individual or business operating a mobile food facility in California. Must be at least 18 years old and have a valid business license. Costs $500-800 and takes approximately 4-6 weeks to complete. This guide covers requirements, documents needed, fees, timeline, and the complete application process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a food truck permit in California?

Processing typically takes 4-6 weeks from the date you submit a complete application. However, plan for 2-3 months total when you factor in plan check review, vehicle preparation, and scheduling the inspection.

Do I need a business license before applying for a food truck permit?

Yes, you must have a valid business license from your city or county before applying for a Mobile Food Facility permit. This is a prerequisite for the application.

How much does a California food truck permit cost?

The permit fee ranges from $500-800 annually, depending on your county. Additional costs include plan check fees ($200-400), inspection fees, and commissary rental which can be $300-800/month.

Can I operate my food truck while waiting for permit approval?

No, you cannot legally operate without an approved Mobile Food Facility permit. Operating without a permit can result in fines of $1,000+ and seizure of your vehicle.

What happens if my food truck fails the inspection?

You'll receive a detailed report of violations that must be corrected. You can schedule a re-inspection once fixes are complete. Re-inspection fees are typically $100-200. Most vendors pass on the second attempt.

Do I need to renew my food truck permit?

Yes, Mobile Food Facility permits must be renewed annually by December 31st. Renewal fees are similar to initial permit costs ($500-800). You'll need to maintain current insurance and business license.

What's a commissary and do I really need one?

A commissary is a licensed commercial kitchen where you prep food and store your truck. Yes, it's required by California law - food trucks cannot prep food at home. You'll need a signed commissary agreement before your permit is approved.
